My summer of 2022 was spent at Saint-Gobain Research North America working for Bonded
Abrasives - Vitrified. I was taken on as the sole mechanical engineer of the group and tasked
to develop a concept for automated manufacturing of pressed abrasive blanks. These blanks
are made by depositing an evenly distributed mixture of abrasive grain and bonding agent
shown in the upper image. The powder is then pressed and fired to form a chemically bonded
blank which can be machined to its final form for precision grinding. Unevenness in the
distribution of grain will lead to inconsistencies in the final product and wasted materials.

I went about solving this problem using a dual-axis linear rail system, controlled deposition
through vibrational agitation, and custom software for generating the optimal distribution
pattern, controlling the linear rails and handling the control logic.

As this project was intended for use in a factory environment, the electronics were all housed
in IP-rated enclosures to prevent ingress of airborne particles. Using DIN-rail mounted
electronics, the electronics panel was kept organized for ease of troubleshooting and
maintenance.v

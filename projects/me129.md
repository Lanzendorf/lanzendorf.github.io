# ME 129: Experimental Robotics

In this introductory robotics course at Caltech, me and a partner built a
two wheeled autonomous rover to navigate a variety of maps, following a
set of expected behaviors. The robot sees the world through three
front-mounted infrared sensors and three ultrasonic sensors mounted
forward, left, and right (not pictured). Control is achieved through a
Raspberry Pi connected to the motor controller and both sets of sensor
hardware through GPIO.

Capabilities of this robot on a given map include autonomously exploring
the map until all intersections are fully explored, drive to an intersection at
a given (x,y) coordinate in the shortest possible path using Dijkstra’s
algorithm, collision avoidance, and publishing map data and location with
ROS to communicate with other robots.
